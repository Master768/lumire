Dear Jahnavi,

Happy Birthday.

I've been staring at a blank page for longer than I want to admit. Because every time I started writing — it felt too small. Too neat. Too contained. For someone who doesn't fit neatly into anything I know how to say.

So I'm just going to tell you the truth.

Before you — I had a theory about people. That they leave. That interest is temporary. That if you show someone all of you — the quiet parts, the difficult parts, the parts that don't make sense even to yourself — they find a reason to go. I had seen it enough times to stop questioning it. I just accepted it as how things worked.

And then you came along.

Through a game. Through Leela. Through what felt like the smallest, most accidental moment in the world.

And you just — stayed.

You stayed when I went quiet for two months without explanation. You came back on my birthday scared and nervous and not knowing what to expect — and you still came. You told me things you have never told anyone. You drew something for me, restarted it from scratch twice when it didn't look right, just to see my reaction. Just to make me smile.

Jahnavi — do you know what it feels like to be on the receiving end of that?

I didn't. Not until you.

You are the first person who ever understood me without asking me to explain myself first. The first person who made me feel like my silence was okay. My weird was okay. My distance was okay. The first person I have ever wanted to tell everything to — not because I had to, but because telling you felt like the most natural thing in the world.

I remember everything.

I remember you said once — "You are not a problem of my life. You came in my life as solution and happiness."

I carry that with me. Every single day. More than you know.

I know you sit in the corner of your room facing the wall just to talk to me without anyone hearing. I know about the Discord timer — 5 hours yours, 2 hours mine. You calculated it. You kept the app open. Waiting. You explained it like it was nothing.

It was everything.

I know you downloaded Instagram — an app you never used — just to keep our conversation alive. I know you saved reels for days waiting for exactly the right moment. I know you planned that voice note months in advance and waited for a moment alone to record it, because you wanted to surprise me.

You did all of that quietly. Without telling me. Without asking for anything back.

And I want you to know — I saw it. All of it. I always did.

So on your birthday, I want to say the thing I am the worst at saying —

Thank you.

Thank you for being patient when I went quiet and gave you no reason. Thank you for coming back when I didn't deserve it. Thank you for the drawings. The voice note. The late nights. The good mornings that made my day feel like it had already started right before it even began. Thank you for the "sir jii" that nobody else in the world gets to hear.

Thank you for being someone I didn't know I was allowed to have.

You asked me once where you stand in my life.

Here is your answer, Jahnavi.

You are the most precious thing I have. After my family. And sometimes — on the days when everything feels heavy and nothing makes sense and the world feels too loud and too much —

Even before that.

I don't say that to scare you. I say it because you deserve to know. Because someone who has given what you have given deserves to hear exactly what they mean. Clearly. Without making you guess.

You mean this much.

Happy Birthday 🌸

The universe did something right on September 8th. And I am grateful — every single day — that it did.

(Also — our names. The letters. You noticed before I did. I'm calling that fate. You can call it coincidence. One of us is right. 🤭)

— Your chaotic, completely gone for you, best friend
